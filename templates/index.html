Fake News Detection
Machine Learning Project

Fake news detection using machine learning is a sophisticated process that leverages advanced algorithms to automatically identify and classify news articles as real or fake. At its core, this approach involves collecting a large dataset of news articles, each labeled as either true or false. The text data is then preprocessed through techniques such as tokenization, stop-word removal, stemming, and vectorization (e.g., TF-IDF or word embeddings) to convert raw text into numerical features

Textual Features

These include word frequency, n-grams, TF-IDF scores, and linguistic patterns that help identify the writing style and structure of news articles.

Metadata Features

Utilizes information such as publication date, author, and source credibility to provide additional context for classification..
